About me (resume)

I’m a quant trader, programmer and part time photographer and researcher. I studied probability and statistics at École polytechnique, Sorbonne University and École des Mines de Nancy.

My main occupation these days is to find patterns in digital assets -during my non compete period-. I also regularly do some landscape shooting, but still not too confident to share them publicly.

My main areas of expertise are computer science and financial economics. Previously, I worked as a quant/researcher (FT/internships) in various companies across different projects:

  • A secretive australian proprietary trading firm, as a junior quant trader in London then Austinmer-Australia- for a bit. (Great chess club and atmosphere in the London office).
  • Ocp Group, the largest worldwide producer of phosphates and its derivatives, as a junior consultant focused on research topics, exciting projects led by MIT professors.
  • JP Morgan a big bank, worked a quant research associate in long dated FX Options Model Validation, PDE methods and Monte Carlo were my best friends.
  • Two-I, a video analytics company applying AI frameworks for security purposes, worked as an assistant researcher on emotion propagation in football games.

On the little free time I have left, I -try- to do research. I am getting very interested by topics such as Pyth Network oracle and real time bidding in various auction mechanisms – and write open source code on github.
