About me (resume)

I’m a quant trader, programmer and part time photographer and researcher. I studied probability and statistics at École polytechnique, Sorbonne University and École des Mines de Nancy.

My main occupation these days is to find patterns in digital assets -during my non compete period-. I also regularly do some landscape shooting, but still not too confident to share them publicly.

My main areas of expertise are computer science and financial economics. Previously, I worked as a quant researcher (Junior/intern) in various companies across different projects:

  • A secretive australian proprietary trading firm, as a junior quant trader in London then Austinmer-Australia for a bit. (Great chess club and ambience in the London office)
  • Ocp Group largest worldwide producer of phosphates and its derivatives, as a junior consultant focused on research topics, exciting projects led by MIT professors.
  • JP MorganQuant research associate in long dated FX Options Model Validation, PdE methods and Monte Carlo were my best friends.

On the little free time I have left, I do research – I am a PhD candidate in Financial Economics and work on ML research with Rob Tibshirani (Stanford) – and write open source code on github.

Previously, I was a competitive programmer who participated in ICPC, the most prestigious programming contest in the world.
My team became Southwestern Europe champion and gold medalist and achieved France’s all-time record at the ICPC World Finals.
